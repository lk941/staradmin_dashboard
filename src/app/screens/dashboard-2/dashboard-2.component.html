Learning Style

ABOUT THIS STYLE

  • Kinesthetic/Tactile learning is a style of learning in which an individual learns most efficiently through participating in activities and doing things.
    Kinesthetic/Tactile learners retain information better when it is delivered through exploring a subject themselves rather than written or audio form.
    A child who is an kinesthetic/tactile learner relies on action and engagement as their primary way of learning. Many kinesthetic/tactile learners may experience challenges when instructions or information are given in written or verbal form but can clearly understand them when they are able to try out the activity for themselves or take notes.

WAYS TO LEARN/ENGAGE

  • Read stories/texts aloud
  • Teach them songs and rhymes to sing
  • Engage them in conversation

Source: https://abilitypath.org/ap-resources/childrens-learning-styles/
